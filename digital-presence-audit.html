The Compounding Function · Defense

Your storefront is rotting and everyone can see it but you.

A buyer’s first impression of an AWC company is formed before a single conversation — on a website, a LinkedIn profile, a YouTube channel. Across the portfolio, those first impressions are frozen in 2022, half-built, or a decade stale. This is an honest scan of what the market sees right now. Some of it is not flattering.

Curtis Leopardo wears three-piece suits. Not costume — respect for an age when men presented themselves with dignity, when how you showed up said something about how seriously you took the work. The world around him forgot that. So did Grand Kahn.
— From the Chicago portfolio review
Why This Matters

A frozen website doesn’t hold the line. It surrenders it.

Silence isn’t neutral. Every quarter a 117-year-old contractor looks abandoned since 2022, a younger competitor with a fraction of the history quietly inherits the authority the incumbent spent a century earning. Brand equity is finite. Neglect hands it to whoever is still willing to show up.

This scan separates two kinds of finding. Active decay — assets that were built, then abandoned, now broadcasting that the company has stopped paying attention. And strategic gaps — a presence that’s maintained but leaves its best story untold. The first is a wound. The second is unclaimed ground. Both are costing money right now.
