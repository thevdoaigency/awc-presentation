02The Day

Built around the meeting, not competing with it.

Nine interviews at 15–18 minutes each is roughly 2.5 hours of camera time — not something to squeeze in before a 12:00 PM meeting starts. The plan is to shoot candid B-roll while people arrive, let the meeting run uninterrupted, then hold the room afterward.

10:00 AM CT
Crew arrival & setup. One interview station in a side room or quiet corner — single backdrop, two-camera setup (wide + close), lav mics, consistent lighting for every subject so the footage cuts together.
11:00–11:55 AM
Candid B-roll as people arrive. Handshakes, coffee, informal conversation — this is where the documentary-feel footage comes from, not the formal interviews. Ask permission but keep it unobtrusive.
12:00–1:30 PM
Meeting runs, uninterrupted. No interviews during this window. If the room allows, one static camera can capture the meeting itself for potential cutaway footage — confirm with AJ first.
1:30–3:45 PM
Interview block — Tier 1 first, then Tier 2, then Tier 3 as time allows. ~15 min per person + 3 min reset between subjects = roughly 18 min per slot, 7 confirmed slots = 2 hrs 6 min. Comfortable room for the Tier 3 Casto follow-up and a buffer for anyone running long.
3:45–4:00 PM
Wrap. Confirm releases signed, back up footage on-site before anyone leaves the building.
Assumption: this schedule assumes the meeting runs roughly 90 minutes (12:00–1:30 PM) and that the room stays available afterward. If the actual meeting length or venue availability is different, the interview block shifts accordingly — confirm the real agenda with AJ's office and I'll adjust the run of show.
03Question Banks

Two universal questions for everyone, then it gets specific.

Every interview opens with the same two questions — that's what makes the footage cut together into one portfolio piece later, not nine disconnected clips. Everything after that is built from what's actually already known about each company, so the answers are usable the same day.

Universal Opener
Ask every single person, identical wording
  1. What does your company do, in one sentence a stranger would actually understand?
  2. What's one thing about your company most people get wrong, or don't know at all?

04Logistics

What has to be true for nine interviews to actually happen.

Crew & Gear

  • Alex Hernandez (DP) + Sammie Hunter (1st AD) minimum — a two-person crew keeps resets under 3 minutes
  • One consistent backdrop and lighting setup for the whole day — every subject should visually match
  • Two cameras per interview (wide + close) to give editors cutaway options without a second take
  • Backup audio recorder in addition to camera audio — no re-shoots possible if a lav fails mid-conversation

Before the Day

  • Confirm the real meeting agenda and end time with AJ's office — this schedule is a placeholder until that's confirmed
  • Send each Tier 1 and Tier 2 subject a heads-up with the two universal questions in advance, so nobody's caught flat-footed on camera
  • Pre-fill release forms where possible to save time on the day
  • Confirm room availability post-meeting — a locked-down quiet space, not a hallway

Running the Room

  • Keep Tier 1 subjects first in the order — if the day runs long, Tier 3 is what gets cut, never Tier 1
  • Have a runner (Raj or equivalent) managing who's next so the interview chair is never empty
  • Flag AJ and Alan's slots to run adjacent to each other — natural continuity between the CEO and CFO answers

Same-Day Wrap

  • Back up all footage on-site before anyone leaves — two drives minimum
  • Log which questions actually got strong answers per subject, so editors know where to start
  • Flag any subject who needs a follow-up shoot instead of trying to force it into this one day
