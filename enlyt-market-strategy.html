01The Market, Briefly

The category is booming. The company is invisible inside it.

Smart building technology is a $150–175B market growing 11–19% a year depending on the forecast — easily the fastest-growing sector any AWC portfolio company sits in. The demand driver is real: IoT-connected security, energy management, and automation are becoming standard specification, not a premium add-on. Enlyt already has the right positioning — three service lines (Design + Engineering, Hardware Installation, Systems Integration), eight named industries, and four defined buyer types (Property Owner, Developer, General Contractor, Building Engineer).

The problem isn't the market or the positioning. It's that Enlyt's actual product — routed cable, configured devices, a working IoT network — is invisible the moment the walls close. Unlike a contractor's finished building or a supplier's physical product, there's nothing to photograph once the job is done. That's the whole strategic problem, and it's a content problem before it's anything else.

The Core Insight
Enlyt can't sell what the buyer can see, because there's nothing left to see. It can only sell what the buyer can be shown — which makes video and motion graphics the primary sales tool, not a marketing nice-to-have.
02The Growth Trajectory

What Enlyt could grow into, once the category is legible.

Enlyt's problem probably isn't sophistication — the Sonder, Melia, Belgravia, and Life Fitness wins prove the technical work is real. The problem is that smart-building integration is a category almost nobody outside it already understands, and right now Enlyt is explaining itself in the same language a general contractor would use. Here's who else started as a hands-on integrator and grew into something bigger — and what each one proves is possible.

The Growth Line
Enlyt grows from "smart building installer/integrator" into the operating layer that makes buildings more intelligent, efficient, secure, and controllable.
Middle-Market Comp

Albireo Energy

The most realistic near-to-mid-term path. Albireo started in 2013 as an independent building-controls integrator, grew through acquisition with PE backing (Huron Capital), and is now the largest independent BAS integrator in the US — $271.6M in revenue, 35,000+ installations, 39 offices, technology-agnostic across every major BAS manufacturer. It proves an independent integrator can scale into a category leader without ever building its own hardware platform.

Founded 2013 · Now largest independent BAS integrator in the US
Category Model

Schneider Electric — EcoStruxure Building

The architectural blueprint for what "the platform" looks like once Enlyt's integrations mature: an open, IoT-enabled system that unifies HVAC, power, lighting, and security into one mobile-enabled view. Not Enlyt's near-term shape, but the proof that unifying disparate building systems into a single interface is a real, valuable category — not just a construction line item.

Open, data-centric building management system architecture
Enterprise Scale Model

Siemens Smart Infrastructure

What this looks like fully matured — fully integrated automation across HVAC, lighting, shades, safety, security, and fire systems, deployed at campus and smart-city scale. Siemens' own framing is worth borrowing: "75% of buildings are not managed as efficiently as they could be" — connectivity and integration are the fix, which is exactly Enlyt's pitch at a much smaller scale today.

Desigo CC + Building X — campus and smart-city scale deployments
AI Platform Archetype

Johnson Controls — OpenBlue

The AI-native version of where this category is heading — a cyber-secured, AI-powered ecosystem that unifies live building data to improve energy efficiency, equipment performance, and occupant experience. Useful less as a near-term target and more as a signal of where buyer expectations are moving: from "installed system" to "intelligent, responsive operating layer."

AI-powered, agent-based building operations interface

None of these four started at their current scale. Albireo is the proof point that matters most — it's the closest analog to where Enlyt stands today, and the clearest evidence that going from "integrator with great case studies" to "category leader" is a matter of making the work legible and repeatable, not reinventing what the work is.

03From Strategy to Deliverable

Five video formats that solve the invisibility problem.

Enlyt already did the strategic thinking for us — it just never turned it into content. The four buyer personas, the five real case studies, and the eight named industries on the site are a ready-made production plan. Here's how each maps to an actual deliverable.

Format 01

The "What You Can't See" Explainer Series

Animated motion-graphics walkthroughs showing what's actually inside the walls — device layouts, network topology, sensor placement — for a 26-story hotel or a single-family retrofit. This is the single highest-leverage format: it's the only way to show the product at all.

Built with: AI-generated motion graphics + Voiceover Studio narration
Format 02

Case Study Video Series

Enlyt already has five written case studies (Sonder Hotel, Melia Hotel, Life Fitness, Belgravia Group, single-family) with a Challenge / Solution / Results structure — a video script sitting in plain sight, never produced. Frame each around a single outcome: comfort, access, energy, control, or security.

Built with: Semantic Bridge (case study text → script) + Shot Builder
Format 03

Persona-Specific Explainers

Four short videos — one each for the Property Owner, Developer, General Contractor, and Building Engineer — answering "why does Enlyt matter to someone in my seat." Enlyt already segments its site this way; nothing on it is filmed yet.

Built with: Voiceover Studio (consistent narrator across all four)
Format 04

Industry Vertical Cutdowns

Eight named industries (aviation, athletic/fitness, corporate, healthcare, hospitality, industrial, retail, MDU) become eight 15–30 second LinkedIn cutdowns, each targeted to the GCs and developers who build in that specific vertical.

Built with: Cutdown tool, sourced from the explainer and case study masters
Format 05

Smart-Building Myth-Busting + Sales Deck Visuals

Short pieces correcting the assumptions non-technical buyers make about smart building tech (cost, complexity, disruption during install), paired with clean system diagrams built for GC and developer sales decks — the same visual language as the explainer series, repurposed for the pitch room.

Built with: Shot Builder diagrams + Voiceover Studio
04The AI Production Stack

Which tool does what, specifically for this company.

This isn't generic content advice — it's a direct map from what already exists in the VDO Aigency toolkit to what Enlyt specifically needs, given a product that can't be filmed in the normal sense.

Semantic BridgeRailway · /board-export
Turns existing case study copy into video scripts automatically. Enlyt's five "Facts at a Glance" case studies (Industry / Client / Challenge / Solution / Results) are already structured like a script outline. Semantic Bridge can ingest that copy and output a first-pass narration script per case study — cutting script-writing time on this series to near zero.
Voiceover StudioRailway · 5 voice IDs, ElevenLabs v3
Gives Enlyt one consistent narrator voice across every format. Since none of Enlyt's content involves an on-camera executive interview yet (unlike Grand Kahn or Leopardo Efficiency), a single branded AI narrator voice can carry the explainer series, the persona videos, and the case studies — establishing sonic consistency before a single human is ever filmed.
Shot BuilderRunway Gen-4.5
Generates the "invisible infrastructure" visuals no camera can capture. Network diagrams, device layouts inside walls, IoT topology maps — this is the format built specifically for content that has no physical footage to fall back on. This is the highest-priority tool for Enlyt of any company in the portfolio.

Turns each long-form explainer or case study into 8 vertical-specific shorts. One case study video becomes reusable across every relevant industry page — a hospitality case study cut down for the Hospitality, Athletic/Fitness, and MDU verticals wherever the underlying system (guest automation, access control) applies.
