Prepared for AJ Leopardo, President · Alexander West Capital

This document outlines the structure of an ongoing brand and content function for the seven Alexander West Capital operating companies, delivered through VDO Aigency as agency of record. It is a companion to the working portfolio brand audit prepared for the June 2026 Chicago review.

00Why This Structure

The portfolio brand audit identifies what each operating company needs. This document defines who delivers it, on what cadence, and under what terms.

Two structural decisions shaped the engagement architecture proposed here.

First, this function lives outside the AWC org chart. The work spans seven operating companies, each with its own leadership, internal teams, and cultural particulars. A vendor relationship to AWC at the family office level is structurally simpler than an internal C-suite role at any one of the operating companies, particularly Leopardo Construction. The brand function reports to AWC leadership through VDO Aigency, not through any operating company's internal hierarchy.

Second, the GATOR equity engagement remains a separate track. GATOR is the only portfolio entity with a venture-style cap table and a defined Series A trajectory. The brand and operational work for GATOR is structured under its own letter of intent — salary, equity, and acceleration terms previously agreed verbally with Curtis Leopardo. That engagement runs in parallel to this one. Neither contaminates the other.

02Scope of the Portfolio Retainer

The portfolio brand function is two things at once. The same operators deliver both, on the same standards, from the same operational discipline.

Offense — The Growth Function

  • Brand identity systems that align the portfolio without erasing distinct operating company voices
  • Case study production from real footage at real customer sites, across active companies
  • Executive thought leadership and founder visibility for portfolio principals
  • Campaign architecture across LinkedIn, podcast, conference, and direct outreach surfaces
  • The portfolio narrative AWC takes to LPs and the family takes to the public

Defense — The Compounding Function

  • Brand consistency across every portfolio touchpoint, channel, and team member
  • RFP response infrastructure that reclaims sales hours and protects pipeline integrity
  • Customer onboarding playbooks that preserve referrals and reduce churn across operating companies
  • Operational standards that scale without adding portfolio-wide headcount
  • Brand asset management and reputational protection across legacy properties

The case for centralization is that both functions happen in the same team, from the same operators, with the same standards. Splitting offense and defense across an external agency and an internal function loses the leverage in both. The Leopardo Efficiency pilot has already proven the model works in practice. The portfolio retainer is the deployment of that proven model across the remaining six companies.

04What’s Not in Scope

Clear boundaries protect the budget, the working relationships, and the function’s credibility inside the portfolio.

  • Operating company management. The retainer informs brand and content; it does not replace any operating company’s executive team or product leadership.
  • Sales execution. Sales hires, sales operations, and revenue accountability stay inside each operating company.
  • Engineering and product development. DFH Connect software, GATOR platform engineering, and any operating company’s technical product roadmap are outside scope.
  • Financial reporting and family office operations. AWC’s family office finance, accounting, and LP reporting functions are not touched by this engagement.
  • Outside agency relationships. Existing agency vendors (BBG and others) may continue in their specific lanes. The portfolio retainer does not require their termination, only their integration into the unified brand standard.
  • Project production work beyond the retainer scope. Case study films, animated explainers, website builds, photography campaigns, and event production are quoted per project at VDO Aigency standard rates, approved by the AWC finance function on a per-SOW basis.

05Compensation Structure

The retainer is structured for cash-flow predictability on both sides. AWC commits annually with monthly cadence visibility. VDO Aigency is paid in advance, by wire, with no deliverable-by-deliverable invoicing of strategic time.

Strategic Retainer $12,000 / month  Brandon’s time on portfolio brand strategy, voice, positioning, executive content direction, and team coordination across active operating companies. Paid in advance, by wire, on the first business day of each month.
Mobilization Payment $24,000 at signing  Two months of retainer prepaid at engagement signing. Non-refundable. Funds the front-loaded intake and audit work of Months 1 and 2, during which no further invoicing occurs.
Production Work Per-project SOW  Case study films, animated explainers, website builds, photography, executive interview series, and any project work beyond the retainer. Approved by the AWC finance function on a per-project basis. Fifty percent due at SOW signing, fifty percent due upon delivery. Seven-day wire terms.
Internal Headcount Separate AWC line  Raj Brahmbhatt, currently a VDO contractor, transitions to direct AWC employment as portfolio media operations manager at approximately $67K base plus benefits. Reports operationally to the retainer engagement; payroll line lives inside AWC, not VDO Aigency.
Term 12 months minimum  With annual renewal at five percent retainer escalation, defined at signing. Either party may terminate with sixty days written notice after Month 6. Retainer continues through the notice period. Mobilization payment retained as severance regardless of termination cause.
Cadence Monthly & Quarterly  Monthly retainer wire on the first business day. Quarterly business review with AJ, Rob, and Curtis — one hour, slide deliverable. Annual renewal review in Month 11.
Payment Architecture · Note for Rob
Wire transfer, prepaid monthly. No Net 30. No deliverable-gated invoicing on the retainer.

Family offices retain senior outside counsel, accountants, and operating advisors with prepaid wire transfers as standard practice. The payment architecture above mirrors that convention. The mobilization payment borrows construction-subcontractor vocabulary — mobilization fee, due at signing, equal to two months of retainer — and operates on the same logic. Cash flow stays clean on both sides; neither party is ever owed work, and neither is ever in float.

07Why This Structure Serves AWC

The decisions encoded in this engagement architecture were each made with a specific outcome in mind.

External vendor, not internal C-suite hire.

The brand function reports to AWC at the family office level, not into any operating company’s internal org chart. This avoids friction with existing Leopardo Construction marketing personnel and operating company executives who would resist a new C-suite peer. The same work gets done with materially less political cost.

Cash-only retainer, equity isolated to GATOR.

The portfolio retainer is cash. GATOR equity is documented separately, on its own terms, with its own structure. Combining them would entangle the family office’s ongoing operating budget with a venture-style cap table conversation. Keeping them parallel preserves clarity for both Rob and AJ — and keeps Series A diligence on GATOR clean.

Wire-prepaid retainer, per-project production approvals.

Net 30 invoicing on a strategic retainer creates floating receivables and ongoing approval conversations. Wire-prepaid retainer with separate per-project production approvals gives the family office finance function clean monthly visibility, eliminates float, and removes the temptation to slow-pay an outside operator who already has portfolio access.

Phased activation with adjustment triggers.

The retainer is calibrated to a Phase 1 scope of three active companies. Activation triggers at five and seven companies adjust the retainer ceiling proportionally. Capacity, quality, and compensation move together; expansion is never absorbed silently by either side.

The proving ground exists.

Leopardo Efficiency has been operating as a pilot of this exact function for months — outreach board, executive voice work, content pipeline, AI-augmented production discipline. The portfolio retainer is the deployment of a proven operating model, not the construction of a theoretical one. Risk to AWC is correspondingly lower than for any greenfield brand engagement.
