02 · State of the Brand Today

The fastest-scaling entity in the portfolio. The newest brand to define.

Page 02

GATOR is unlike the other five portfolio companies in one structural way: there is no decades-old brand to surface, no buried wins to dig out, no archive of project work to translate. GATOR is being built in real time. Every brand decision made in the next ninety days will shape what the market believes about the company for the next decade.

Five cities have closed before formal entity formation: Terre Haute IN, Miami-Dade County FL, South Miami FL, Evansville IN, and Doral FL. The pipeline is moving faster than the brand. The audit is not a diagnosis of damage — it is a chance to set the brand correctly the first time, instead of repairing it later.

What works.

Velocity
Five municipal customers signed before entity formation. Nothing in the AWC portfolio has scaled this fast. The pre-formation sales velocity is the single most important credibility signal GATOR has — for procurement directors, for Series A investors, and for the AWC family office itself.
Differentiated Mechanic
The blind reverse auction is not a feature — it is a structural rethink of how municipal procurement works. Most gov-tech sells better dashboards. GATOR sells a different transaction. That difference compounds in trust and in pricing power.
Inherited Credibility
The endorsement line “A Leopardo Technology” is the strongest single asset GATOR has at this stage. It says GATOR is not a venture-funded gov-tech experiment — it is the procurement technology of a family that has been working with municipalities since 1977.

What does not.

No Brand Yet
GATOR is being sold faster than it is being branded. A procurement director closing in week six receives a different proposal than one closing in week sixteen. Without a brand system, that inconsistency compounds.
No Marketing Surface
A buyer searching “municipal procurement software” today does not find GATOR. The five closed customers were closed through relationships, not search. That is correct for the founder-led stage. It does not scale past it.
No Visible Founder Voice
GovTech is bought on trust in the team. The CEO has the relationships and the credibility. Neither is publicly visible at the volume Series A diligence will require in 4 to 8 months.

05 · The Brand Gap

Series A diligence will not be patient with an unfinished brand.

Page 05

A company that closes five cities pre-formation should not arrive at Series A still building its first deck.

Founder-led sales got GATOR to five customers. Series A institutional capital will demand a different kind of evidence: case studies that scale beyond a CEO conversation, a brand surface that supports a sales team, executive thought leadership that signals category authority, and a customer narrative that compounds across cities without the founder in the room. None of that exists yet. All of it can be built in the runway available before the round.

What founder-led sales produces
Five closed cities, 27% average savings, a defensible mechanic, and a procurement leader audience that trusts the CEO personally. This is what got GATOR here. It does not scale to fifty cities.
What Series A diligence requires
Documented customer outcomes with named procurement directors. Demonstrated category authority through published thought leadership. Brand defensibility against existing gov-tech competitors. Sales infrastructure that operates without the founder in every conversation.
What the timing demands
The brand and content infrastructure for the next stage takes 4 to 6 months to build correctly. The Series A window is 4 to 8 months out. The work has to start now, or the round arrives without the evidence to defend the valuation.
What the alternative costs
An undermarketed Series A round closes at a lower valuation. Each million dollars of foregone valuation translates directly to dilution the founders absorb. Brand investment now is the cheapest way to protect the cap table.
06 · Recommendations

Six moves over the first 90 days.

Page 06

GATOR is the audit where the work is forward-building rather than backward-surfacing. The recommendations are calibrated to the Series A window.

01

Lock the GATOR brand system.

Wordmark, color, typography, voice, and lockup variants. The endorsement line “A Leopardo Technology” integrated as a permanent component. A brand book that any sales deck, pitch, RFP response, or piece of collateral inherits from. The foundational deliverable. Everything else depends on it.

02

Produce five case study films.

One per closed city. 60-90 second cinematic films built from on-camera interviews with the procurement directors who signed. Real numbers, real names, real outcomes. Distributed across the website, sales decks, LinkedIn, and the Series A data room. Each one becomes an asset that closes the next ten cities.

03

Build the founder voice.

The CEO becomes the public category authority. Three LinkedIn posts per week, monthly bylines in gov-tech publications, occasional speaking at municipal conferences. The goal is not personal brand — it is establishing the founder as the person procurement directors recognize before the sales call begins.

04

Build the gov-tech content engine.

Procurement directors, city managers, and chiefs of staff search for specific topics: cooperative purchasing, reverse auctions, group health procurement, fleet aggregation, IT category sourcing. GATOR should publish one substantive article per topic per month, written by leadership, distributed through procurement networks. Authority compounds. Search visibility compounds. Pipeline compounds.

05

Architect the sales surface.

Sales deck, RFP response template, one-pagers by vertical (supplies, health, fleet, IT), demo videos, conference materials. The sales asset library that lets a senior AE close without re-explaining the company every time. Built once, used by every salesperson hired in the next two years.

06

Adopt the AWC portfolio identity system.

GATOR is positioned as “A Leopardo Technology” on day one. The portfolio identity system makes that endorsement structural, not decorative. Shared typography, shared design language, shared content standards. GATOR inherits decades of family operating credibility without compromising its own product identity.

07 · Proposed Positioning
The procurement technology built from inside government work.
A line that names the lineage and the audience in one breath. GATOR was not built by Silicon Valley engineers studying procurement from a distance. It was built by operators who have worked with municipal governments for decades. Works on a website, on a Series A deck, and at the top of every proposal.
