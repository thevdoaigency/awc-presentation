02 · State of the Brand Today

The wins exist. The website is a frame around them with the picture missing.

Page 02

Enlyt has installed smart building technology for Sonder, Melia, Belgravia, and Life Fitness. These are flagship references in hospitality, multi-dwelling residential, and athletic facilities — categories where smart-building tech is hardest to deploy and most often fails.

A buyer landing on the current website does not learn any of this. The homepage shows section headers for “Outcomes,” “Solutions,” and “Case Studies” that are placeholder-empty. The case studies that do exist are buried in a sub-navigation labeled “Our Work.” Each one runs three paragraphs and resolves before it tells the story.

What works.

Client Roster
Sonder, Melia (ME Miami), Belgravia Group, Life Fitness. Three of the four are international or national brands. The fourth (Belgravia) is one of Chicago’s most established luxury residential developers.
Capability Architecture
Three clear capability tracks: design & engineering, hardware installation, systems integration. Eight industry verticals named (aviation, athletic/fitness, corporate, healthcare, hospitality, industrial, retail, MDU). The taxonomy is correct.
Buyer Targeting
Four named buyer types: property owner, real estate developer, general contractor, building engineer. This is sophisticated — most smart-building companies talk to one of these and confuse the rest.

What does not.

Half-Built Homepage
Multiple homepage sections sit empty. The first impression of a smart-buildings firm is a website that does not work. Every other signal Enlyt sends is undermined by this one.
Buried Proof
The Sonder case study lives at enlyt.io/index.php/our-work/hospitality-sonder-hotel — four clicks from the homepage. It runs three paragraphs and shows only thumbnail-sized photos. A Sonder install is the kind of work that justifies premium pricing. It needs to be the front door.
No Voice
No executive presence. No content marketing. No LinkedIn activity from Enlyt leadership on the topics buyers actually search (BMS retrofits, IoT integration, hospitality automation ROI). A technical buyer cannot find this team.
03 · Historical Wins

Four flagship installs that should anchor every page of the site.

Page 03

These are the projects buyers should see in the first ten seconds. Currently they require sustained effort to find.

Hospitality · Flagship

Sonder Hotel, Chicago Loop

26 floors · fully automated guest experience

Enlyt coordinated the location and documentation of every connected device in a 26-story building — including IP and MAC addresses for routers, beacons, and relays. The build delivered a hotel that operates without on-site staff facilitating guest engagement. Hospitality is the toughest vertical for smart-building tech because guest-facing failures kill review scores. Enlyt cleared that bar at scale.

Hospitality · International

ME Miami by Melia

International luxury brand · flagship Miami property

Melia is a multinational luxury hospitality brand. Landing a flagship ME-branded property in Miami means surviving the procurement process of an international hotel group. That qualifying bar — not just the install — is the credential.

Residential · Chicago Anchor

Belgravia Group

Luxury MDU residential · Chicago’s premier developer

Belgravia is one of Chicago’s most established luxury residential developers. Multi-dwelling units (MDU) have completely different smart-building requirements than hospitality — resident privacy, individual unit control, building-wide infrastructure. Doing both well, for a Belgravia-grade developer, demonstrates technical depth across vertical types.

Athletic / Fitness

Life Fitness

National fitness brand · athletic facility integration

Athletic facilities require different infrastructure than offices or hotels: heavy equipment, locker rooms, member-facing technology, high-traffic patterns. Life Fitness is a Fortune-500-tier client. Smart-building integration for them validates Enlyt’s ability to design for end-user complexity, not just building systems.

04 · The Brand Gap

A first impression problem, costing a Tier 1 firm Tier 3 buyer leads.

Page 04

A firm that wired a 26-story Sonder property is being judged on a half-finished homepage.

Smart-buildings is a category where buyer trust is built on visible technical sophistication. A real estate developer, a building engineer, or a general contractor evaluating smart-building vendors will spend less than ninety seconds on a homepage before deciding whether to proceed. Enlyt’s ninety seconds currently show empty section headers and a sub-navigated case study library. The contrast between actual capability and presented capability is the largest gap in the portfolio.

What the homepage promises
Five section headers, four of them empty. A taxonomy of capabilities that hints at depth but does not show it. A “Building Upgrade Estimator” that competes for attention with case studies that should outrank it.
What the work actually shows
Hospitality-grade systems integration at scale. Multi-vendor device coordination. Successful deployments across four industry verticals for international and national brands.
What buyers do instead
Move to the next vendor on their shortlist. Building engineers and developers rarely give a smart-building firm a second look. The half-built site is a quiet eliminator, not a passive presence.
The compounding cost
Each Sonder, Melia, or Belgravia install should become the seed for the next ten deployments. Without case studies, content, and visibility, every flagship install is a closed loop — not a brand asset.
05 · Recommendations

Six moves over the first 90 days.

Page 05

All six are scoped to be completed without a full site rebuild. The existing taxonomy is sound. The work is to fill it and surface what is buried.

01

Fix the homepage first.

Fill every empty section. Lead with Sonder. The homepage should answer three questions before scroll: who Enlyt works with, what kind of work they do, and why a developer or building engineer should call them. Each question gets one section. None should require a click to resolve.

02

Produce four cinematic case study films.

Sonder. ME Miami. Belgravia. Life Fitness. Each one a 60–90 second film built from real install footage and on-camera interviews with the property owners or operators. Distributed across the website, LinkedIn, sales decks, and direct outreach to developers. The case studies become the brand — not supporting evidence for it.

03

Build the technical-buyer content engine.

Building engineers and developers search for specific technical solutions: BMS retrofits, hospitality IoT integration, MDU smart-building ROI. Enlyt should publish one technical article per month per topic — bylined by leadership, indexed by Google, distributed on LinkedIn. The audience is small and specific. The intent is high.

04

Surface the buyer-type architecture.

The current site names four buyer types (property owner, developer, GC, building engineer) but does not differentiate the experience for each. A property owner cares about ROI and uptime. A general contractor cares about install timelines and coordination. Each one should land on a tailored page that speaks to their specific decision criteria.

05

Introduce a face to the brand.

Smart-building integration is bought on trust in the people doing the work. A senior Enlyt engineer or principal becomes the public voice — LinkedIn presence, technical bylines, occasional podcast appearances, an annual industry talk. Buyers in this category research the team before the technology.

06

Adopt the AWC portfolio identity system.

Enlyt currently presents as an independent firm with no visible connection to the broader Alexander West Capital portfolio. Aligning typography, color, content infrastructure, and tone across the portfolio produces immediate cohesion — and inherits trust from Leopardo Construction’s decades of work with the same buyers Enlyt is selling to.

06 · Proposed Positioning
Hospitality-grade smart buildings.
Engineered, installed, and integrated.
A line that names the most demanding category Enlyt has cleared, and frames the three capabilities the firm actually owns end-to-end. Works on the homepage, in an RFP cover letter, and at the top of every proposal.
