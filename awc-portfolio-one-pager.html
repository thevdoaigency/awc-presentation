Portfolio Brand Function
A 90-Day Initiative
For Company Presidents

AWC is beginning a 90-day portfolio brand and content initiative with Brandon Cole and VDO Aigency. Many of our companies grew up inside or alongside Leopardo Construction — real history, real capabilities, real customers. That gave us credibility, but several companies are now at the stage where they need to become more visible, more legible, and more active in the market on their own. This is about helping those companies leave the nest and win more third-party customers.

The Principle
A Contractor

A Supplier

A Software Company
needs demos
A Public-Sector Seller
needs trust & proof

One centralized function gives us consistency, production capacity, brand discipline, and speed — but the strategy changes by company. This is not a one-size-fits-all marketing program.

The First 90 Days
  1. Audit every company's current brand, website, channel, and content presence.
  2. Identify the right channel strategy for each company.
  3. Activate the first priority companies with content production and publishing.
  4. Build usable sales and marketing assets from existing footage, interviews, and projects.
  5. Distinguish what each company actually needs — proof, product support, education, demos, or trust.
  6. Establish a repeatable portfolio content system that continues past day 90.
What We'll Need From You
  • Access to existing project photos, videos, brochures, proposals, and sales materials
  • A short interview or working session
  • Input on priority customers, verticals, and growth targets
  • Examples of recent wins, recurring customer problems, or misunderstood capabilities
  • A point person to help gather company-specific context

The ask will be practical and limited. This isn't busywork — it's extracting the story, proof, and value that already exist inside your company.

Why This Matters
The AWC portfolio already has the hard part: operating companies with history, customers, capabilities, and proof. The gap is that much of that proof isn't visible, organized, or being used consistently in the market. One portfolio. Seven companies. Different strategies for each. One centralized brand function making the work visible, usable, and repeatable.
